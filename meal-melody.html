The Problem

You don't always know what's in your own kitchen.

People buy groceries and forget. Things get pushed to the back of the fridge, stacked in the pantry, lost behind something else. Eating out has become so convenient that cooking from what you already have feels like extra work to figure out.

Most recipe apps don't help with this. They assume you know what dish you want and search forward from there. They surface generic recommendations regardless of what's actually in your kitchen, and they treat dietary preferences as a filter that narrows the list rather than a starting point that shapes the recipe.

Meal Melody starts from the other end. Scan your pantry or fridge. See what's actually there, including the things you forgot. Then get recipes adapted to your diet, restrictions, and taste — with a personal AI chef walking you through it step by step, multilingual and voice-enabled so you don't need clean hands to follow along.

The Approach

Start from what's in the kitchen, then adapt.

The MVP is built around a pantry and fridge scanner that surfaces what's actually there — including the things you bought and forgot. From that ingredient pool, a Gemini-powered recommendation layer suggests recipes adapted to your diet, restrictions, and taste preferences instead of just filtering generic recipes down.

An AI recipe assistant sits inside the cooking flow, handling real-time substitutions, multilingual support, and voice-enabled step-by-step guidance. The flow is built so you can stay in the cooking, not in the app.

Shipped solo on Lovable for UI scaffolding, Supabase for backend services, Claude Code for engineering, and GitHub for version control.
